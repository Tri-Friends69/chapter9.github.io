Use of communication technologies for the intention of harming another person or in other words "usse of internet service and mobile technologies such as web pages, discussion groups as well as instant messaging or SMS text messaging with the intention of harming another person either emotionally or mentally is called cyber bully.

Cuberbullying is when a child tortured, threated, harassed, humiliated, embarrassed or otherwise targeted by another child using the Internet, interactive and digital technologies or mobile phones .

WHY CHILDREN CYBERBULLY?

  • It is often because of anger, revenge or frustration.
  • Many children bully other children just for fun, entertainment or they are bored.
  • It has been observed that some children resort to bully because of too many tech toys available to them and thus they misuse the technology.
  • Many bully others just to get a reaction from the person being bullied as they enjoy teasing others
  • However, at times children do it unknowingly, as they either send a message to the wrong recipient or didn't think before doing something like this.
  • The attention seekers do it to tormet others and to satisfy their own ego.

    • EFFECTS OF CYBERBULLYING

    A child can be cyberbullied anywhere, this can happen at home as well as at school, 24 hours a day due to intensive use of technology. Severe, long-term, or frequently cyberbullying can leave both victims and bullies at greatest risk for anxiety , dpression and other stress-relatd disorders. In some rare cases, some children resort to suicide.

    HOW TO PREVENT CYBERBULLYING

    cyber bullying can be prevented by:
  • Educating about the consequences(losing their ISP or IM accounts) of cyberbullying
  • Teaching them to respect others privacy and to take a stand against bullying.
  • Blocking the bully as most devices have settings that allow us to electronically block emails, IMs, or texts from specific people .
  • Limitting access to technology under complete parental supervision and guidance.
  • Sharing online friends with parents "to follow" on social media sites.
  • Learning various ways to be safe online that is by safeguarding passwords and by not posting the exact location, address or whereabouts when outside home.
  • Ensuring emails, messages etc. are sent to the right place , i.e to the right email address
  • By not forwading other people's e-mails without their permission or sharing their personal information
  • By not wasting peoples' time or bandwidth with junk, chain e-mails and false rumore
  • By not attacking others online, that is by anything that could be considered insulting or that is controversal.
  • Not indulging in cyber fights by writing the e-mail, instant message or post in anger

    These types of crime may be :
  • Hacking : Fraud and Financial crimes and thefts
  • Copyright infringement
  • Child pornography amd child bullying
  • Harassment and crime against women.
  • Other cross-border crimes , cyber terrorism.
    • Cyber Law: Cybercrimes can involve criminal activites, such as theft, fraud, forgery, defamation and mischief, all of which are addressed by the information Technology Act,2000.
